## Question 4 

One situation that stands out was during my time at Gilbarco Veeder-Root. I had done an analysis on operational alarm data and built a report that was technically accurate and well-structured. The client found the report useful and the insights were valid.

However, I realised afterwards that I had not communicated the urgency of the findings effectively. Some of the issues I identified required immediate attention, but the way I presented them made them seem more like general observations rather than critical risks. As a result, the impact of the analysis was understated.

From that experience, I learned that good analysis is not only about correctness, but also about how clearly you communicate importance and business impact. Since then, I make a deliberate effort to highlight risk, prioritise key findings, and clearly indicate where action is required.


## Question 5 

In one project, I worked with a colleague from another department where we were jointly responsible for delivering reports to a client. The challenge was that they relied on manual reporting methods and were hesitant to adopt business intelligence tools, even though the client required more interactive and insight-driven outputs.

Rather than working around the issue, I took a more proactive approach. I built a working example using BI tools to demonstrate how the reporting could be improved and how it would benefit the client directly. This made the value more practical and easier to understand. I also walked through the process step-by-step and aligned the output with what the client actually needed.

At the same time, I ensured that deadlines were still met by taking ownership of the more technical parts where necessary. Over time, this improved both the quality of the reports and the overall approach to delivering insights.



## Question 6 

In my role, there are regular situations where I need to deliver analysis within tight timelines, especially when supporting client reporting. One example involved preparing a detailed report on site performance and alarm trends within a limited timeframe.

To manage this, I broke the work into clear stages, starting with data extraction and cleaning, followed by analysis and then presentation. I prioritised the parts that would have the biggest impact first, and used tools like Python and SQL to automate repetitive tasks and speed up the process.

I also made sure to communicate progress early so that any adjustments could be made before final delivery. This helped avoid last-minute issues and ensured the output remained aligned with expectations.

This approach allowed me to deliver a high-quality result on time, and reinforced the importance of structuring work efficiently and focusing on what delivers the most value under pressure.
